ABOUT

Short Bio

Mega Buana (b. 1999, Bandar Lampung, Indonesia) is a choreographer and performance artist based in Indonesia. She received an MA in Dance Creation from the Indonesian Institute of the Arts (ISI) Surakarta.

Her practice investigates intimacy, relationality, and embodied negotiation through choreographic research. Using shibari as a choreographic methodology, she explores how tension, restriction, weight, and mutual support shape movement, perception, and new forms of bodily relation, approaching care as an active and embodied choreographic practice.

Artist Statement

My artistic practice develops through choreographic research into how relationships are experienced through the body. I create physical conditions that invite bodies to negotiate tension, proximity, dependency, resistance, and mutual support.

Shibari functions in my practice as a choreographic methodology rather than a visual aesthetic. Through binding, weight-sharing, and shifting points of tension, I investigate how physical conditions shape movement, perception, trust, care, and vulnerability.

My work approaches care as an active choreographic practice rooted in attentiveness, reciprocity, and shared responsibility. Rather than resolving tension, I am interested in how bodies continuously adapt to one another, revealing choreography as an evolving process of negotiation and radical care.
